CCSS First Year Representative Election

All first-year CCSS members (Carleton students enrolled in a CS program) may vote for this year's CCSS First Year Representatives. Polls open at 12:01 AM on Thursday 3 October 2013 and close at 11:59 PM on Friday 4 October 2013.

Breaking News (Oct 3rd 13:41): There were issues with the voting server earlier today. They should be resolved now. If you have any issues, please notify the CCSS President: Simon Pratt <president@ccss.carleton.ca>.

Vote now

The electoral system

You will be asked to fill in a preferential ballot: in other words, rank the candidates. The candidate you prefer most should be #1, then your next preferences in decreasing order. Don't rank candidates you don't support.

Votes will be counted according to BC-STV (explanation), which is essentially a generalization of instant-runoff voting.

Voting is anonymous. You will be asked to log in to an authentication service run by the School of Computer Science to verify that you are an eligible voter, and then returned to the CCSS voting server. The SCS does not pass on your identity.
